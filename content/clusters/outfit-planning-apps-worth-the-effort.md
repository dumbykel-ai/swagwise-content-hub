## The Honest Caveats

### When Outfit Planning Apps Aren't Worth It

**Very small wardrobes (<50 items)**
If you own few items, you probably remember them all. The visibility benefit is minimal. Physical organization may suffice.

**Zero interest in clothing**
If you genuinely don't care about what you wear (uniform lifestyle, identical items daily), the optimization benefits are wasted.

**Unable to complete setup**
If you won't photograph your wardrobe (even gradually), you won't get value. The system requires the initial investment.

**Wrong app choice**
Bad apps waste time and create frustration. The 78% abandonment rate is largely due to poor app quality, not the concept. **App selection matters enormously.**

### When Outfit Planning Apps Are Definitely Worth It

**Large wardrobes (75+ items)**
More items = more value from visibility and organization.

**Daily outfit decisions feel stressful**
If mornings are frustrating, apps provide immediate relief.

**Frequent duplicate purchases**
If you often buy things you already own, prevention pays for itself.

**Want to wear more of what you own**
If 50%+ of your wardrobe goes unworn, apps unlock that value.

**Value your time**
If 73 hours annually is meaningful to you, apps are clearly worth it.
